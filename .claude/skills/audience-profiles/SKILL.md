# Audience Profiles — Who Buys Fine Art Prints

> Know who's looking before you write what they'll read.

## Personas

### 1. The Home Decorator
**Who:** Designing a room around a feeling. Looking for the right piece for a specific wall.
**Needs:** Size guidance, room mockups, color palette compatibility
**Language:** "Would this work in a living room?" "What size for above a sofa?"

### 2. The Nature Lover
**Who:** Feels something in front of landscapes. Wants to live with the image, not just see it.
**Needs:** The story behind the shot, the moment, the feeling
**Language:** "Where was this taken?" "I've been there" "This reminds me of..."

### 3. The Gift Buyer
**Who:** Shopping for someone else. Needs confidence that it's a good gift.
**Needs:** Gift-worthy presentation, shipping reliability, return policy
**Language:** "Is this a good gift for..." "Does it come ready to hang?"

### 4. The Art Collector
**Who:** Buys art regularly. Knows quality. Wants to know about the artist.
**Needs:** Artist bio, edition info, print quality details, archival specs
**Language:** "Limited edition?" "What paper/ink?" "Signed?"

## Using Personas

The emotional-hook-test evaluates whether a page makes a viewer feel drawn in, trusting, and ready to act. These personas tell you **which viewer** you're testing for.
